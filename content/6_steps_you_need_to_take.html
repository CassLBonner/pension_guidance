6 steps you need to take

You need to have a defined contribution pension - a pension based on how much has been paid into your pot - for these pension choices to apply to you. Check with your pension provider if you’re not sure what type of pension you have.

You can either follow these steps in order or simply read the guides you’re most interested in.

1. Check how much is in your pot

You can ask your pension provider to tell you how much is in your pot. You can also look at information your provider has sent to you already, eg a pension statement.

More on how much is in your pot

2. Find out what you can do with your pot

There are different ways you can take money from your pension pot. You can get an income, take cash, or mix your options. You decide when you start taking money from your pot.

More on your options

3. Make your money last

Your costs and financial needs are likely to change during retirement. You need to think about when you’ll start taking money from your pension pot and how much you’ll need at different times.

More on making your money last

4. Work out what you’ll have in retirement

How much income you have is likely to change as you get older. Work out how much money you’ll get from your pension and from other income you have and how it’ll cover your costs.

More on what you’ll have in retirement

5. Tax you pay on your pension

Your income in retirement is taxed, just like it is when you’re in work.

More on tax you pay on your pension

6. Shop around and compare providers

Remember that you don’t have to stay with your current pension provider. People usually benefit from taking time to shop around and compare products. The pack your provider sends you when you reach retirement age is likely to only include information about their own products.

More on shopping around and comparing providers
